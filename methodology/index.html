Calculator methodology

Transparent formulas and review flags

Advertisement

Tax-year scope: Calculator defaults use 2026 federal planning assumptions reviewed July 13, 2026. State and local rates remain user-entered.

Official sources used

  • IRS Topic No. 554 for self-employment tax fundamentals
  • SSA Contribution and Benefit Base for the 2026 wage base and Social Security rates
  • IRS Topic No. 560 for Additional Medicare Tax
  • IRS Notice 2026-10 and Announcement 2026-11 for the two 2026 business mileage periods
  • 2026 Form 1040-ES and Publication 505 for estimated-tax education and standard dates
  • IRS simplified home-office guidance

Formula review process

Official values are stored in data/tax-years/2026.js with effective dates, verification status, review notes, and source URLs. Shared formulas live in assets/js/calculator-core.js; calculator controllers should not duplicate federal constants.

Annual update process

Run the tax-config validator, follow ANNUAL-TAX-REVIEW.md, update effective periods for midyear changes, then rerun calculator and website validation before publication.

Rounding policy

Calculations retain decimal values. Money is normally displayed to the nearest dollar so a planning estimate does not imply return-level precision.

Known exclusions

The tools do not prepare returns, determine eligibility, calculate complete federal brackets, model every deduction or credit, determine worker classification, or establish estimated-tax penalty protection.

State-tax limitations

No state rate is supplied. Any state or local percentage is a user-entered cash-planning assumption, not state-specific tax logic.

User-entered assumptions

Federal income-tax percentages, state/local percentages, benefits, deductions, credits, withholding, prior payments, and custom mileage rates require the user’s own support and may receive different tax treatment.

Correction policy

Material source or formula errors should be corrected promptly in the canonical tax-year data, shared formula, affected page copy, validation fixtures, and review date. No result is described as IRS-approved.

Last reviewed: July 13, 2026.
