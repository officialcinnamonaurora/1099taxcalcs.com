    What Is Self-Employment Tax?

    Understand the Social Security and Medicare tax commonly associated with net earnings from self-employment.

    Last reviewed: July 14, 2026

    Plain-language introduction

    Understand the Social Security and Medicare tax commonly associated with net earnings from self-employment. This guide separates general planning concepts from facts and tax-year rules that need current official verification.

    Why this matters for 1099 workers

    Independent workers usually do not have an employer withholding payroll taxes from client payments, so understanding this separate Social Security and Medicare tax is essential to cash planning.

    Step-by-step explanation

    1. Start with gross business income.
    2. Subtract supported business expenses to estimate net profit.
    3. Apply the Schedule SE net-earnings method.
    4. Calculate the Social Security portion within the remaining wage base.
    5. Calculate Medicare and review Additional Medicare considerations separately.
    6. Estimate federal income tax as a separate calculation.

    Key ideas

    • Self-employment tax is separate from federal income tax.
    • Net business profit is an important starting point.
    • W-2 wages can affect the remaining Social Security wage base.

    Hypothetical example

    Hypothetical example: A worker with $60,000 of receipts and $12,000 of supported expenses starts with $48,000 of preliminary business profit. This illustration is not an actual taxpayer outcome or a promise of tax treatment.

    Common mistakes

    • Confusing gross receipts with net earnings
    • Treating the deduction for part of SE tax as a refund
    • Using an old tax-year value or deadline without checking the current official source.

    What this guide does not cover

    What Is Self-Employment Tax? focuses on its stated planning question. It does not calculate every credit, deduction, special tax, state or local rule, accounting method, entity rule, penalty exception, or eligibility limitation, and it does not prepare or file a return.

    When professional help may be appropriate

    Professional review may be useful when the what is self-employment tax? question involves multiple businesses, workers, inventory, depreciation, entity elections, multistate activity, notices, amended records, or facts outside the linked calculators.

    Records to gather

    Use invoices, payment statements, receipts, mileage or workspace records where relevant, prior returns, withholding statements, contracts, and estimated-payment confirmations.
