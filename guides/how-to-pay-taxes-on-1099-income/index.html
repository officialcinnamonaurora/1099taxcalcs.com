How to Pay Taxes on 1099 Income

Organize withholding, estimated payments, filing, and recordkeeping for independent-work income.

Last reviewed: July 14, 2026

Plain-language introduction

Organize withholding, estimated payments, filing, and recordkeeping for independent-work income. This guide separates general planning concepts from facts and tax-year rules that need current official verification.

Why this matters for 1099 workers

Client and platform payments may arrive without federal, state, or payroll withholding, leaving the worker responsible for records, projections, payments, and annual filing.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Record gross income from invoices, platforms, cash, and other sources.
  2. Track supported business expenses and personal-use allocations.
  3. Estimate self-employment tax.
  4. Estimate federal income tax separately.
  5. Enter state and local assumptions separately; this site supplies no state rate.
  6. Consider estimated payments or additional household withholding.
  7. Reconcile forms and records when filing the annual return.

Key ideas

  • Reconcile income records before estimating profit.
  • Keep income tax and self-employment tax distinct.
  • Use official payment channels and retain confirmations.

Hypothetical example

Hypothetical example: A freelancer reviews year-to-date profit, withholding, and prior payments before planning the remaining reserve. This illustration is not an actual taxpayer outcome or a promise of tax treatment.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting for a form before recording income
  • Sending an estimate without retaining proof
  • Using an old tax-year value or deadline without checking the current official source.

What this guide does not cover

This guide does not calculate every credit, deduction, special tax, state or local rule, accounting method, entity rule, penalty exception, or eligibility limitation. It does not prepare or file a return.

When professional help may be appropriate

Qualified help may be useful for multiple businesses, employees or subcontractors, inventory, depreciation, entity elections, multistate work, amended records, large income changes, notices, or facts that do not fit the linked calculators.

Records to gather

Use invoices, payment statements, receipts, mileage or workspace records where relevant, prior returns, withholding statements, contracts, and estimated-payment confirmations.
