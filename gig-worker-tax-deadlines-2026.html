2026 Quarterly Deadlines

The 4 Quarterly Estimated Tax Due Dates

Gig workers and freelancers don't have an employer withholding taxes, so the IRS requires you to pay taxes yourself four times a year. Miss one and you face an underpayment penalty — even if you pay everything by April.

Q1 Payment — 1st Quarter
April 15
2026
Covers income earned: January 1 – March 31, 2026
Pay estimated taxes on all gig income earned in the first three months of 2026. This deadline also coincides with the annual return deadline for 2025 taxes.
⚠️ Also: 2025 return due today
Q2 Payment — 2nd Quarter
June 16
2026
Covers income earned: April 1 – May 31, 2026
Note: Q2 covers only 2 months (April–May), not 3. The IRS's uneven quarter structure catches many gig workers off guard. June 15 falls on a Sunday in 2026, so the deadline shifts to June 16.
📅 Only 2 months covered
Q3 Payment — 3rd Quarter
September 15
2026
Covers income earned: June 1 – August 31, 2026
Q3 covers three months of summer income. This is often the highest earning period for delivery drivers and rideshare workers. Set a reminder now — mid-September sneaks up fast.
☀️ Summer income deadline
Q4 Payment — 4th Quarter
January 15
2027
Covers income earned: September 1 – December 31, 2026
Q4 covers the holiday season — peak earnings for many gig workers. The payment isn't due until January 15, 2027. You can skip this payment if you file and pay your full 2026 return by February 1, 2027.
🎄 Holiday income — due Jan 2027
⚠️ The IRS quarter structure is uneven and confusing. Q2 covers only April–May (2 months). Q3 covers June–August (3 months). Q4 covers September–December (4 months). This isn't a bug — it's how the IRS has always structured estimated tax periods. Make sure you're paying on the right schedule.

Annual Tax Return Deadline — 2025 Taxes

📋

2025 Tax Return (Form 1040 + Schedule C)

Your annual income tax return for all income earned in 2025 is due April 15, 2026. This includes all gig income, freelance earnings, and W-2 wages. File Schedule C for business income/expenses and Schedule SE for self-employment tax.

April 15
2026

Extension to October 15, 2026

Not ready to file by April 15? You can file Form 4868 for an automatic 6-month extension. This pushes your filing deadline to October 15, 2026. However:

🚨 An extension to file is NOT an extension to pay. If you owe taxes, you must still pay your estimated balance by April 15, 2026. Filing an extension but not paying triggers late payment penalties and interest from April 15 forward. Pay what you owe by April 15, then use the extension just for paperwork.

Avoiding Penalties

Safe Harbor Rules — Pay This Much to Avoid All Penalties

Even if you can't calculate your exact tax owed, you can guarantee penalty protection by meeting one of the IRS's "safe harbor" thresholds. Meet either rule and you owe no underpayment penalty — period.

Safe Harbor Rule What You Pay Best For
Rule 1: 90% of Current Year Tax Pay at least 90% of what you'll owe for 2026, spread across 4 quarters Gig workers who can estimate this year's income fairly accurately
Rule 2: 100% of Prior Year Tax Pay the same total tax you paid in 2025, split evenly across 4 quarters Best if 2025 AGI was $150,000 or under — simple and predictable
Rule 3: 110% of Prior Year Tax Pay 110% of your 2025 tax, split evenly across 4 quarters Required if your 2025 AGI was over $150,000
✅ Rule 2 (100% of prior year tax) is the easiest strategy for most gig workers. Look at line 24 of your 2025 Form 1040 (total tax). Divide by 4. Pay that amount each quarter. You're fully protected from underpayment penalties regardless of how much you actually earn in 2026.

How to Split Safe Harbor Payments Across Quarters

Split your total safe harbor amount evenly — 25% each quarter:

  • Q1 (April 15): 25% of annual safe harbor total
  • Q2 (June 16): 25% of annual safe harbor total
  • Q3 (September 15): 25% of annual safe harbor total
  • Q4 (January 15, 2027): 25% of annual safe harbor total
📌 Example: Your 2025 total tax (Form 1040 line 24) was $4,800. Under Rule 2, your safe harbor is also $4,800. Split across 4 quarters = $1,200 per payment. Pay $1,200 on each of the four due dates and you're fully penalty-protected, even if you earn much more in 2026.
Penalty Info

What Happens If You Miss a Deadline?

There are two different penalties gig workers face — one for missing quarterly payments and one for filing your return late.

Underpayment Penalty (Missing a Quarterly Payment)

Penalty Rate How It Works
Underpayment Penalty ~8% annually (2026) Charged on the unpaid amount from each quarterly due date until you pay. Even if you pay everything in April, the IRS charges interest on missed quarterly amounts from those earlier dates.
How Calculated Daily compounding The IRS charges the federal short-term rate + 3 percentage points. For 2026 this is approximately 8%. It's applied per quarter, per missed payment.
Avoidance Meet safe harbor If you paid enough via safe harbor rules (90% of current year or 100%/110% of prior year), no penalty applies at all — even if you owe a balance in April.

Late Filing & Late Payment Penalties (Missing the April 15 Annual Deadline)

Penalty Rate Cap
Failure to File 5% of unpaid tax per month Max 25% of unpaid tax
Failure to Pay 0.5% of unpaid tax per month Max 25% of unpaid tax
Both Together 5% per month combined (failure to file reduced when failure to pay also applies) Max 47.5% combined
Interest ~8% per year on unpaid balance No cap — continues until paid
🚨 The failure-to-file penalty is 10x worse than failure-to-pay. Even if you can't pay what you owe, always file your return on time (or file an extension). Filing without paying triggers only the 0.5%/month late payment penalty. Not filing at all triggers the brutal 5%/month failure-to-file penalty on top of everything else.
How to Pay

How to Actually Make Your Quarterly Tax Payments

The IRS has several ways to pay. The easiest is the IRS Direct Pay system — free, fast, and no account required.

1

IRS Direct Pay (Recommended — Free)

Go to irs.gov/directpay and pay directly from your bank account. Select "Estimated Tax" and the appropriate tax year. No registration needed. You get an immediate confirmation number. This is the safest and most direct option.

2

IRS Online Account

Create an account at irs.gov/account to see your full payment history, outstanding balances, and schedule future payments. Requires identity verification but gives you the most control.

3

IRS2Go App

The IRS's official mobile app lets you make Direct Pay payments from your phone. Available on iOS and Android. Useful if you want to pay on the go right before a deadline.

4

Electronic Federal Tax Payment System (EFTPS)

Best for gig workers who want to schedule all four payments in advance. Go to eftps.gov and enroll (takes about a week to set up). Once enrolled, you can schedule all four 2026 payments at once so you never miss a deadline.

5

Mail a Check (Form 1040-ES)

Old school but still works. Download Form 1040-ES from irs.gov, fill in the payment voucher, write your SSN and "2026 Form 1040-ES" on the check, and mail to the IRS address for your state. Allow 5–7 business days for delivery before the deadline.

💡 Pro tip: When using IRS Direct Pay or EFTPS, you'll be asked for the "reason for payment." Select "Estimated Tax" and the tax year 2026. This ensures the payment gets applied to the right period and not your annual return balance.
How Much to Pay

How to Calculate Each Quarterly Payment

You have two main methods for figuring out how much to pay each quarter:

Method 1: Estimate Current Year Income (More Accurate)

Estimate your total 2026 gig income, subtract your expenses and deductions, calculate your expected tax, and pay 25% each quarter. This method pays closest to what you actually owe — minimizing both overpayment and underpayment. Use our Quarterly Tax Calculator to do this automatically.

Method 2: Use Last Year's Tax Bill (Simpler)

Look at line 24 of your 2025 Form 1040. Divide by 4. Pay that amount each quarter. You're guaranteed penalty protection under the safe harbor rules, regardless of what you actually earn in 2026. This is the easiest method if your income varies a lot month-to-month.

⚠️ If you're new to gig work and have no prior year tax bill to reference, use Method 1. Set aside approximately 25–30% of every paycheck in a separate savings account, then pay quarterly from that reserve.
