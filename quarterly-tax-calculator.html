Step by Step

How to Actually Pay Quarterly Taxes

Once you know your amount, paying is simple. The IRS offers three free payment methods — no paper checks needed.

Step 1

Go to IRS Direct Pay

Visit IRS.gov/payments → click "Make a Payment" → select "Estimated Tax" and tax year 2026. No account or login required.

Step 2

Verify your identity

IRS will ask for your name, SSN, date of birth, and address. It cross-checks with a prior year return. Have your 2025 tax return handy.

Step 3

Enter payment amount

Enter the quarterly amount from the calculator above. Select your payment date (on or before the deadline). Choose bank account (ACH) — it's free.

Step 4

Save your confirmation

Screenshot or print the confirmation number. At tax time, report quarterly payments on Form 1040 Line 26 — this reduces your final tax bill.

📱 Alternative: Use the free IRS2Go app (iOS/Android) to pay in under 2 minutes. Also accepts credit cards via Pay1040.com (1.87% fee — usually not worth it unless you're earning card rewards).

2026 Payment Schedule

All 2026 Quarterly Tax Deadlines

The IRS sets four payment deadlines each year. These apply to everyone who expects to owe $1,000 or more in federal tax for the year — including gig workers, freelancers, and independent contractors.

QuarterIncome Period CoveredDue DateStatusNotes
Q1 2026 January 1 – March 31 April 15, 2026 Passed Same day as regular tax filing deadline
Q2 2026 April 1 – May 31 June 16, 2026 Passed Note: only 2 months, not 3
Q3 2026 ⚡ June 1 – August 31 September 15, 2026 Pay Now Use calculator above for your amount
Q4 2026 September 1 – December 31 January 15, 2027 Upcoming Or file your return by Feb 1, 2027 instead
⚠️ Q2 is only 2 months long (April–May), not 3. This catches many gig workers off guard. The June 16 deadline covers income earned in April and May only — not June. June income is reported in Q3.

What If a Deadline Falls on a Weekend or Holiday?

If the deadline falls on a weekend or federal holiday, it automatically moves to the next business day. The June 16, 2026 deadline is already adjusted for this — June 15 falls on a Sunday.

Avoid Penalties

The Safe Harbor Rule — Your Penalty Shield

The IRS will never charge you an underpayment penalty if you meet either of these two safe harbor thresholds — even if you end up owing more at tax time:

Safe Harbor RuleWho It Applies ToHow to Qualify
90% Rule Everyone Pay at least 90% of your current year's actual tax liability through quarterly payments
100% of Prior Year AGI $150,000 or less Pay 100% of your prior year (2025) total tax bill, split evenly across 4 quarters
110% of Prior Year AGI over $150,000 Pay 110% of your 2025 total tax bill, split across 4 quarters
✅ The easiest approach: Use Method 2 (last year's tax) in our calculator above. Take your 2025 Form 1040 Line 24 amount, divide by 4, and pay that each quarter. You're 100% penalty-proof — even if your income doubles this year.

What Is the Underpayment Penalty?

If you don't meet a safe harbor and underpay, the IRS charges interest on the shortfall. For 2026, the underpayment rate is 8% annually (the federal short-term rate + 3 percentage points). It's calculated per quarter — so missing Q1 is more expensive than missing Q4.

On a $2,000 underpayment for a full quarter, the penalty is about $40 — not catastrophic, but avoidable.
