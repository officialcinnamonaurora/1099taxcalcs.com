    Self-Employed Health Insurance for Self-Employed Work

    Use this guide to organize potentially relevant costs while keeping personal use, eligibility, reimbursement, and timing limitations visible.

    Last reviewed: July 14, 2026

    What this category generally includes

    potentially eligible health, dental, and qualifying long-term-care premiums may qualify when the facts establish an appropriate business connection and current requirements are met.

    Potentially relevant business expenses

    • potentially eligible medical premiums
    • dental and vision premiums
    • certain qualified long-term-care premiums for eligible people

    These are organizational examples, not a determination that a particular cost is deductible.

    Personal-use and mixed-use considerations

    Personal coverage can potentially produce an income-tax adjustment only when all requirements are met; it is not automatically a Schedule C operating expense. A reasonable allocation needs records that explain the chosen business percentage.

    Reimbursement considerations

    Premium tax credits, marketplace adjustments, employer subsidies, reimbursements, and refunds should be retained and reviewed together. Spending and reimbursement should not be used to claim the same economic cost twice.

    Recordkeeping suggestions

    Keep policy documents, monthly premium statements, proof of payment, coverage dates, covered-person details, marketplace forms, subsidy records, and net-profit calculations.

    Expenses requiring special treatment

    Eligibility depends on facts, employer-subsidized coverage can limit treatment, net-profit limitations may apply, and marketplace premium-tax-credit interactions can require Form 7206 or professional review.

    Capitalization and depreciation warning

    Long-lived property, improvements, acquired intangible rights, prepaid costs, and other capital items may not receive the same current treatment as routine operating costs. This guide does not calculate depreciation, amortization, basis, or an expensing election.

    Eligibility limitations

    Eligibility depends on the complete facts, applicable tax year, entity, business purpose, personal use, reimbursement, timing, and current instructions. The site cannot determine legal deductibility.

    Hypothetical example

    Hypothetical example: A freelancer keeps premium statements and tests eligibility separately from Schedule C operating expenses. The example illustrates record organization and does not promise eligibility or tax savings.

    Common mistakes

    • Assuming every potentially eligible medical premiums payment qualifies without reviewing business purpose.
    • Claiming a personal, reimbursed, or mixed-use amount in full.
    • Keeping only a bank description instead of supporting purpose and treatment.
    • Assuming a $1,000 expense normally creates a $1,000 refund; a deduction generally reduces an income measure rather than reimbursing the purchase price.
