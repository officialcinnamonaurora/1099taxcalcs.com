The Basics

What Is Self-Employment Tax?

Self-employment tax (SE tax) is the Social Security and Medicare tax that every 1099 worker, freelancer, and independent contractor must pay. When you work a regular W-2 job, your employer pays half of these taxes for you. When you work for yourself, you pay the entire 15.3%.

This trips up millions of gig workers every year. You see $60,000 in income and think your taxes will be similar to an employee earning $60,000 — but they're significantly higher because of SE tax.

Who Has to Pay SE Tax?

You must pay SE tax if your net self-employment earnings are $400 or more in a year. This applies to:

  • Rideshare drivers (Uber, Lyft)
  • Delivery drivers (DoorDash, Instacart, Amazon Flex, Grubhub)
  • Freelancers (Upwork, Fiverr, direct clients)
  • Independent contractors of any kind
  • Anyone receiving a 1099-NEC or 1099-K form
  • Side hustle income over $400 net — even without a 1099

Step by Step

How to Calculate Self-Employment Tax

The IRS uses a specific formula to calculate SE tax. Here it is broken down into 5 simple steps — the same formula our calculator uses above.

// Step 1: Net Profit
Net Profit = Gross Income − Business Expenses

// Step 2: SE Tax Base (IRS rule: 92.35% of net)
SE Base = Net Profit × 0.9235

// Step 3: SE Tax
SE Tax = SE Base × 0.153

// Step 4: Deduct half of SE tax from income
Adjusted Income = Net Profit − (SE Tax ÷ 2)

// Step 5: Apply standard deduction, then income tax brackets
Federal Income Tax = brackets(Adjusted Income − Standard Deduction)

1

Track your income and expenses all year

Save every receipt. Log every business mile. The IRS taxes your net profit, not your gross income. Every dollar of expenses reduces your SE tax by 15.3 cents and your income tax by your marginal rate.

2

Set aside 25–30% of every payment

Move it immediately to a separate savings account. Do not touch it. This covers your SE tax plus income tax. If you earn $1,000 from Uber, transfer $250–300 to your tax savings account that same day.

3

Pay quarterly — not just in April

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more, the IRS requires quarterly estimated payments. Missing them triggers a penalty even if you pay everything in April. The four 2026 deadlines are April 15, June 16, September 15, and January 15, 2027.

4

File Schedule SE with your 1040

At tax time, you report your self-employment income on Schedule C (profit/loss from business) and calculate SE tax on Schedule SE. These attach to your Form 1040. Most tax software handles this automatically.
