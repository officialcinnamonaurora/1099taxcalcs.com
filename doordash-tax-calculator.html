DoorDash & Taxes

How DoorDash Taxes Actually Work

DoorDash treats all Dashers as independent contractors, not employees. This means DoorDash pays you 100% of your earnings without withholding a single dollar for taxes. No federal tax withheld. No state tax withheld. No Social Security. No Medicare.

This is both good and bad. Good because you get full control of your money. Bad because you are responsible for calculating and paying your own taxes — and the bill is usually bigger than people expect.

💡 The 25-30% rule: As a Dasher, set aside 25–30% of every payment you receive for taxes. If you earn $200 on a Saturday, immediately transfer $50–60 to your tax savings account. This prevents the April surprise that hits so many first-year Dashers.

Does DoorDash Send Me a Tax Form?

Yes — DoorDash sends two forms depending on how you were paid:

  • 1099-NEC: If you earned $600 or more in Dasher pay (base pay + promotions). Shows your total non-customer payments.
  • 1099-K: If customers paid you $5,000+ through the app (tips via DoorDash payment processing). Threshold drops to $600 starting 2026.

You may receive both forms. If you earned less than $600, DoorDash may not send a form — but you still owe taxes if your net profit exceeds $400.
